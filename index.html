    Biography

    I am a senior undergraduate student in Information Security, Shanghai Jiao Tong University. I work as an undergraduate researcher at Machine Vision and Intelligence Group (MVIG), led by Prof. Cewu Lu.

    Currently, I am a research intern at UC Berkeley, working with Prof. Bo Li and Prof. Dawn Song. During the internship, I do some explorations on secure AI and reinforcement learning, collaborating with Prof. Gerald Friedland, Dr. Ruoxi Jia and Prof. Yang Liu, fortunately.

    I am applying for Ph.D. programs which start in Fall 2019. My research interests involve Machine Learning, Security and Computer Vision, especially the intersections of them.

    Working Papers

    Reinforcement Learning with Perturbed Rewards
    Jingkang Wang, Yang Liu and Bo Li
    In Submission to 7th International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2019
    [arXiv] [code]

    We introduce an unbiased estimator of reward in reinforcement learning which guarantees risk minimization without any assumptions on the true distribution and theoretically study the convergence and finite sample complexity.

    One Bit Matters: Understanding Adversarial Examples as the Abuse of Data Redundancies
    Jingkang Wang, Ruoxi Jia, Gerald Friedland, Bo Li and Costas J. Spanos.
    In Submission to 7th International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), 2019
    [paper] [code]

    We propose a model for adversarial examples consistent with related work, physics and information theory, and prove that input redundancy is indeed a necessary condition for being able to generate adversarial examples.

    The Helmholtz Method: Using Perceptual Compression to Reduce Machine Learning Complexity
    Gerald Friedland, Jingkang Wang, Ruoxi Jia, Bo Li and Nathan Mundhenk.
    In Submission (arXiv:1807.10569)
    [arXiv] [code]

    We propose to reinterpret the Helmholtz free energy formula to explain the relationship between content and noise for sensor-based data and verify perceptual compression can be utilized to reduce ML complexity.

    Multiple Character Embeddings for Chinese Word Segmentation
    Jingkang Wang, Jianing Zhou and Gongshen Liu.
    In Submission (arXiv:1808.04963)
    [arXiv] [code]

    We leverage both semantic and phonetic meanings of Chinese characters in NLP tasks by introducing Pinyin Romanization and Wubi Input embeddings and achieve the state-of-the-art performance in AS and CityU corpora with F1 scores 96.9 and 97.3.
